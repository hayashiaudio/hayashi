# Hayashi Privacy Policy

**Last Updated:** 2026-05-12

## 1. Introduction

At **Hayashi** ("we," "us," or "our"), we respect your privacy and are committed to protecting your personal information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and disclose information when you use our collaborative music lab application offered as a Discord Activity (the "Service").

By using the Service, you consent to the collection and use of information in accordance with this Privacy Policy. **If you do not agree with this Privacy Policy, please do not use the Service.**

## 2. Information We Collect

### 2.1 Information from Discord
Because Hayashi operates as a Discord Activity, we receive certain information from Discord when you launch the Service within a Discord channel. This may include:

- **Discord User ID**: A unique identifier for your Discord account;
- **Discord Username and Display Name**: Your public Discord identity;
- **Avatar Hash**: Reference to your Discord avatar image;
- **Guild (Server) and Channel IDs**: Identifiers for the Discord server and voice channel in which you launched the Activity;
- **Discord Access Token**: A temporary token used to authenticate your session, which we validate with Discord's API.

We do not collect your Discord password, email address, or direct messages.

### 2.2 Information You Provide Directly
When using the Service, you may voluntarily provide:

- **Audio Files and Samples**: Music, sounds, recordings, or other audio content you upload to the asset library or project canvas;
- **Project Data**: Node configurations, signal graphs, patch metadata, arrangement data, and project snapshots;
- **User Preferences**: Settings related to audio engine parameters, UI layout, and workspace customization.

### 2.3 Automatically Collected Information
We automatically collect certain technical information when you use the Service:

- **Device and Browser Information**: Browser type and version, operating system, device type, and screen resolution;
- **IP Address**: Used for network routing, security monitoring, and abuse prevention;
- **WebSocket Connection Metadata**: Session timestamps, connection duration, and room participation events;
- **Crash and Error Logs**: Diagnostic information when the Service encounters errors, including stack traces and context (without audio content);
- **Performance Metrics**: Audio engine latency, rendering times, and synchronization events, collected in an anonymized form where possible.

### 2.4 Cookies and Local Storage
The Service uses browser technologies to store data locally on your device:

- **Local Storage / IndexedDB**: Used to cache project data, audio assets, and UI preferences for offline resilience and faster load times;
- **Session Cookies**: May be used to maintain WebSocket authentication state during an active session;
- **No Third-Party Tracking Cookies**: We do not use cookies for cross-site tracking or advertising purposes.

## 3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

### 3.1 Providing and Operating the Service
- Authenticating your identity via Discord;
- Enabling real-time collaboration and project synchronization between users in the same channel;
- Rendering and processing audio within your browser;
- Storing and retrieving project snapshots and asset metadata from our servers;
- Displaying presence awareness (who is in the room, cursor positions, selection state).

### 3.2 Improving the Service
- Diagnosing technical issues and optimizing audio engine performance;
- Understanding usage patterns to inform feature development;
- Conducting research and analytics on an aggregated and anonymized basis.

### 3.3 Communication
- Sending you critical service announcements, security alerts, or policy updates;
- Responding to your support requests or feedback submissions.

### 3.4 Security and Compliance
- Detecting, preventing, and addressing fraud, abuse, security risks, and technical issues;
- Enforcing our Terms and Conditions;
- Complying with applicable legal obligations.

### 3.5 Legal Basis for Processing (GDPR / EEA Users)
If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), our legal basis for collecting and using your personal information depends on the specific context:

- **Performance of a Contract**: Processing necessary to provide the Service you requested;
- **Legitimate Interests**: Processing for security, fraud prevention, and service improvement, balanced against your rights;
- **Consent**: Where we rely on consent (e.g., for optional analytics), you may withdraw it at any time.

## 4. How We Share Your Information

### 4.1 With Other Users
When you participate in a collaborative session, certain information is inherently shared with other users in the same Discord channel:

- Your Discord username, display name, and avatar;
- Your cursor position, node selections, and presence status;
- Project data, node configurations, and uploaded audio assets that are part of the shared workspace.

**Please be aware that any audio content or project data you add to a shared channel may be accessible to all current and future participants in that channel.**

We may share information with third-party vendors and service providers who perform services on our behalf, such as:

- Cloud hosting and infrastructure providers (e.g., server hosting, database services);

These providers are contractually obligated to use your information only as necessary to perform services for us and in accordance with this Privacy Policy.

### 4.3 Legal Requirements
We may disclose your information if required to do so by law or in response to valid requests by public authorities (e.g., a court order or government agency). We may also disclose your information if we believe in good faith that such disclosure is necessary to:

- Comply with a legal obligation;
- Protect and defend our rights or property;
- Prevent or investigate possible wrongdoing in connection with the Service;
- Protect the personal safety of users of the Service or the public;
- Protect against legal liability.

### 4.4 Business Transfers
If we are involved in a merger, acquisition, financing, reorganization, bankruptcy, or sale of assets, your information may be transferred as part of that transaction. We will notify you (e.g., via email or a notice within the Service) of any such change in ownership or control of your personal information.

## 5. Data Retention

We retain your information for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this Privacy Policy, unless a longer retention period is required or permitted by law.

- **Project Data and Audio Assets**: Retained until you delete the project, the Discord channel is deleted, or we conduct routine data cleanup;
- **Discord Authentication Tokens**: Retained only for the duration of your active session and discarded immediately upon disconnect;
- **Logs and Diagnostic Data**: Typically retained for up to 90 days, after which they are deleted or anonymized;
- **Backup Data**: May be retained for a reasonable period as part of our disaster recovery procedures.

## 6. Data Security

We take reasonable measures to protect your information from unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. These measures include:

- Encryption of data in transit using TLS/SSL protocols;
- Secure WebSocket (WSS) connections for real-time collaboration;
- Access controls and authentication checks for server-side resources;
- Regular security assessments and dependency updates.

**However, no method of transmission over the Internet or method of electronic storage is 100% secure.** While we strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your information, we cannot guarantee its absolute security.

## 11. Audio Processing and Locality

**Important Notice on Audio Data:**

- **Local Processing**: Real-time audio synthesis, effects processing, and WAV rendering are performed entirely within your web browser using technologies such as the Web Audio API, Tone.js, and WebAssembly modules. Raw audio buffers are not transmitted to our servers during normal playback or rendering;
- **Asset Uploads**: When you upload audio files (e.g., samples, recordings), those files may be transmitted to our servers for storage and sharing with collaborators in your channel;
- **Project Synchronization**: We synchronize project metadata, node graphs, and asset references—not raw rendered audio—via our WebSocket and HTTP APIs to enable collaboration;
- **Export Functionality**: When you export a project to a WAV file, that rendering occurs locally in your browser, and the resulting file is downloaded directly to your device.
